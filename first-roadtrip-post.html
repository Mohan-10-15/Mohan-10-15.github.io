An Epic Road Trip to Kodaikanal with Old Friends

October 14, 2025
← Back to Blog

It was still dark outside when we finally gathered, dragging our overloaded bags and sleep-heavy limbs towards the waiting Thar. Seven of us, best friends since fourth standard, all buzzing with the sort of electricity that comes only when you know the trip ahead will be one for the memory books. Our Mahindra Thar 4x4 looked powerful and proud, ready to ferry us out of Chennai and into the heart of the hills. What made this trip different from just any journey, though, was the simple fact of who I was going with: my closest friends. We’ve been together for more than seven years, friends who’ve stuck through thick and thin, from our shy bench-sharing days to wild teenage years and now, as college students facing the world’s chaos.

Leaving the city behind, our journey started on NH48. The streets were just waking up as sunlight crept over the rooftops, painting everything in a soft, golden haze. The playlist for the morning was a mix of childhood nostalgia and new discoveries—each song linked to a memory, every laugh echoing the times we shared benches, secret talks, or lunch breaks in dusty classrooms. That first stretch out of Chennai, my heart felt so full, it was as if I could already see every memory we’d make before we even reached the hills.

We paused at Aasife Biryani House in Vellore for breakfast, reliving vivid memories of school lunches as we savored spicy chicken biryani and mutton curry. It’s funny how old friends can make even an ordinary meal feel like a reunion; jokes about whose tiffin was weirdest or spiciest flew around. Walking next door to Sundaram Tea Stall, we cupped hot chai in our hands and watched early morning life — newspaper boys, chattering school kids, our laughter mixing with theirs. The tea stall auntie joked we looked “just like schoolboys on a field trip,” and honestly, we felt exactly like that.

Onwards we drove, with pit stops for snacks, bathroom breaks, or just to marvel at a sunrise slicing through the mist. Reaching Dindigul, we feasted at The Royal Dhaba. Chicken fry and crispy kebabs, more jokes, some stories about crushes and embarrassing PT period memories. Someone spiked their water with lime and salt, just as we used to during sweaty sports days. It was like nothing had changed, even though life pushed us to different colleges and even new cities.

The Palani Hills marked the real start of our adventure. The Thar climbed hairpin bends with ease, towering pines and wildflowers greeted every turn, and mist curled through the valley like a living thing. It’s no exaggeration to say this felt like magic. Our first true taste of Kodaikanal was at Kodai Boat Lake, a place straight out of an old Tamil movie. We rented paddle boats, rowed in wild zigzags, dared each other to sing our old school anthem in the middle of the lake, and ended up laughing so hard we nearly tipped the boat.

Every day became an exploration. We wandered through Pine Forest—all of us pausing to inhale the earthy, resinous air, the ground soft under our feet, pine cones everywhere. The sunlight slipped quietly through straight trunks, and, suddenly, silly competitions broke out: who could balance a pine cone on their head, or somersault without getting covered in needles. There’s a peace in those woods you can’t find anywhere else—a kind of quiet that lets you hear your own heart beat, or your best friend’s whispered memories.

We visited the legendary Shooting Spot, a meadow famous from countless movie scenes. With mountains stretching behind us, we became filmmakers for the day, reenacting silly scenes, running up the slopes, and collapsing into the grass to watch the clouds drift by. Laughter echoed off the hills, and our old group chant—something silly from primary school—started up without planning, as if the mountains had called those words out of us.

Pepper Falls was our first big, wet adventure. We trekked through thick green undergrowth, slipping and scrambling over rocks, until we reached the roar of the water. The spray glittered in the sunlight and, within minutes, shoes and t-shirts were off and we were under the falls, getting drenched, screaming just for the thrill of it. Someone tried to pose for the “coolest waterfall shot” but slipped mid-pose, and that photo will forever be our trip’s favorite meme.

The legendary Guna Cave was next—a place as dark and mysterious as the stories claim. Some of us were hesitant (okay, I was definitely chicken), but peer pressure and the lure of adventure drew us in. The cool, damp air, echo of unknown depths, jokes about spotting ghosts, and narrow shafts of light made us feel like explorers. Even standing at the mouth, we shivered from excitement and the faint fear that always comes when you try something new, together.

One of the most peaceful, breathtaking stops was Bison Point. Standing at the edge, we watched sea-like waves of mist drift past, unveiling layer after layer of blue-green hills. We spent ages there, no one needing to talk, just breathing in views that went on forever, arms around each other, feeling ten feet tall but humble all at once. Maybe the world is huge, but friendships like ours make any place feel like home.

If you ask anyone in our group for a favorite moment, someone will mention Gundar Falls. The path there was wild—a little muddy, a little wild, but totally worth it. We climbed over slippery rocks, found pools of the coldest, freshest water, and took turns jumping, splashing, and (occasionally) wiping out. Sunlight danced through the falls and onto our shivering, happy faces as we snapped pictures and promised never to let distance—or adult life!—pull us apart.

We also squeezed in a visit to Berijam Lake, a rare treat on a clear afternoon. The silence, the stillness of blue water surrounded by tall trees, the occasional rustle of wildlife somewhere in the woods made all of us drop our voices in awe. We sat for a while, just taking it in, and someone quietly started talking about their hopes and worries for the future—no pretense, just honesty, held safe by the friendship we’d built over all these years.

Nights in Kodaikanal became something out of a childhood fantasy. Back at our hillside guesthouse, we made sure to light a campfire every night. Those flames lit up everything: faces eager for stories, hands passing plates, shared grins glowing brighter than the embers. On the second night, determined to make the trip epic, we bought meat and veggies at the market and held our own BBQ. Picture a group of college guys bundled in jackets and blankets, huddling around a playful fire, grilling chicken skewers (slightly burnt, but a team effort!), making spicy potatoes on the side, and laughing about who was the worst cook. The food was simple, but the experience of preparing it together, struggling with stubborn coals or missing salt, made everything absolutely perfect.

Later on, we made omelets in a battered skillet, cooked noodles, and even tried to whip up a makeshift biryani in a big pot. I don’t know if anything tasted as great as that meal, because it was seasoned with memories and joy. Sharing our own made food under the stars, telling ghost stories, singing school songs, daring each other to reveal crushes, or spilling dreams about the future—those were moments of pure belonging.

By the last day, our clothes were dirty, eyes heavy, but spirits wide awake. Even as we packed our bags, there was a sense that something special had happened, something we’d carry for the rest of our lives. The drive back down the mountain was subdued but content—a soft, grateful silence, hands gently drumming on the Thar’s dashboard, eyes gazing at the retreating hills. We took one last pitstop at Sundaram Tea Stall, sipping quietly as if to make the moment last a little longer, knowing full well that we’d all be back in “real life” soon—exams, projects, internships, the endless hustle.

The journey back to Chennai felt shorter, and by the time we rolled past the city’s edge, we knew that our friendship, already tested by years and strengthened by thousands of small moments together, had just gained a new treasure. Seven school friends who’d stuck together through laughter, insanity, heartbreak, victory, and random crushes, now had the wild, unforgettable story of Kodaikanal—the rivers we swam in, the fires we cooked over, and the hills that heard our dreams.

If you made it to the end of this, I hope you remember: sometimes, the most beautiful places are just an excuse for the world’s greatest company. Go find your people, pack your bags, and let the road carry you somewhere unforgettable. Because those are the stories you’ll tell forever.
